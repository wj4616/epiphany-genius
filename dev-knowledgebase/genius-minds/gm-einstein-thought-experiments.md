## Documented Evidence

Einstein's thought experiments (Gedankenexperimente) are the most thoroughly documented examples of systematic visual scenario construction in scientific history. At age 16, he formulated what would become Special Relativity through a self-reported visualization:

> "A paradox upon which I had already hit at the age of sixteen: If I pursue a beam of light with the velocity c... From the very beginning it appeared to me intuitively clear."

On the equivalence principle (1907), Einstein described the pivotal moment:

> "I was sitting on a chair in my patent office in Bern. Suddenly a thought struck me: If a man falls freely, he would not feel his weight."

Einstein himself described his cognitive process as "combinatory play" — constructing visual mental scenarios before any mathematical formulation. He watched himself "in his mind's eye as he rode on a light wave." Mathematics followed insight; it did not precede it.

### Cognitive Structure

The Gedankenexperiment methodology has an identifiable four-step structure:

1. **Construct a physically extreme scenario**: Push a variable to a limit (velocity → c, gravity → zero, acceleration → maximum). Extremes reveal structure not visible in ordinary conditions.
2. **Apply existing laws**: Ask what currently accepted theory predicts in this scenario.
3. **Identify the contradiction or paradox**: If the scenario exposes a logical contradiction between two accepted principles, that contradiction IS the discovery.
4. **Infer the resolution principle**: The resolution of the contradiction produces the new theory. The contradiction is not a problem — it is the signal.

## Cognitive Mechanism

Einstein's thought experiments embody **contradiction-driven reasoning via visual simulation**: construct a scenario → run it mentally → find where current theory breaks → extract the correction.

Three features distinguish this from ordinary reasoning:

**Visualization before formalization**: Einstein reported that mathematical notation came after the visual understanding was already complete. The thought experiment produced an insight; the math was the translation. This inversion (insight → formalism, not formalism → insight) is the critical amplifiable feature.

**Reduction to essentials**: Each thought experiment strips the problem to its minimal components — one train, one lightning bolt, one reference frame. The reduction is deliberate: removing irrelevant variables forces the essential tension to become visible.

**Scaling and transformation**: Einstein routinely asked "what happens at the limit?" — as velocity approaches c, as gravitational field approaches zero, as the observer accelerates to match the field. Limits and transformations reveal invariances invisible at normal scales.

**Self-as-observer**: Einstein placed himself inside the thought experiment ("I am riding the light wave," "I am in the falling elevator"). First-person embodiment of the scenario was a consistent feature, not an occasional tool. This is structurally different from third-person analysis.

## AI/Machine Amplification

Human thought experiments are limited by:
- Working memory (can hold 1-2 simultaneous variables)
- Imagination bandwidth (scenarios stay near familiar experience)
- Anchoring on first plausible scenario (confirmation tendency)

An LLM can amplify each limitation:
- **Variable scaling**: Systematically apply extreme values to every parameter simultaneously, not just the one the human focused on
- **Observer multiplication**: Run the same scenario from multiple observer frames in parallel
- **Contradiction detection**: After constructing the scenario, formally check whether any two accepted principles predict contradictory outcomes — this check can be exhaustive rather than intuitive
- **Scenario inventory**: Generate 10-15 scenario variants before selecting the one with maximum tension, rather than running one scenario and anchoring on it

The core machine amplification is **scenario multiplication with systematic contradiction scanning**: what a human does once, intuitively, for the scenario that happens to occur to them, the machine does exhaustively across a parameterized scenario space.

## Skill Mapping

**Protocol: Einstein Thought Experiment Mode**

1. **Extreme scenario construction**: "Identify the core variables in this problem. For each variable, construct a scenario where it is pushed to its physical or logical extreme (maximum, minimum, zero, infinity, limit). State the scenario in first-person terms: 'I am the observer who...'"

2. **Theory collision scan**: "Apply all currently accepted principles or assumptions to this extreme scenario. For each principle, state its prediction. Identify any pair of principles whose predictions contradict each other in this scenario. The contradiction IS the signal — do not resolve it prematurely."

3. **Contradiction extraction**: "State the contradiction precisely: 'Principle A predicts X; Principle B predicts Y; X and Y cannot both be true in this scenario.' Name what assumption would need to be false to resolve the contradiction."

4. **Inversion step**: "Assume the contradicted assumption IS false. What new principle replaces it? Restate the problem from within this new framework. Does the contradiction dissolve?"

5. **Observer frame multiplication**: "Now run this scenario from 3 different observer positions. Does the contradiction persist from all frames? Does it vanish from one? The frame where it vanishes reveals the invariant structure."

**Trigger condition**: Use Einstein mode when a problem contains two apparently accepted principles or constraints that produce different predictions for the same situation. The conflict is the entry point.

**Minimal version** (single-step invocation): "Construct an extreme scenario that maximally stresses this problem's assumptions. Place yourself inside it as first-person observer. State what two accepted principles predict. If they conflict, the conflict is the answer."
