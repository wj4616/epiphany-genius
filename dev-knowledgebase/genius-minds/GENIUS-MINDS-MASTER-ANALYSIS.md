## Section 1: Individual Profiles

### 1.1 Einstein: Thought Experiment Methodology

**Documented Cognitive Traits:**

1. **Contradiction-driven mental simulation** — SOURCE: Direct quote: "A paradox upon which I had already hit at the age of sixteen: If I pursue a beam of light with the velocity c... From the very beginning it appeared to me intuitively clear." SOURCE: Wikipedia inventory of 11 documented thought experiments.

2. **Visualization before formalization** — SOURCE: "Mathematics followed insight; it did not precede it." Einstein described his cognitive process as "combinatory play" — constructing visual mental scenarios before any mathematical formulation.

3. **Reduction to essentials** — SOURCE: "Each thought experiment strips the problem to its minimal components — one train, one lightning bolt, one reference frame."

4. **Scaling and transformation** — SOURCE: "Einstein routinely asked 'what happens at the limit?' — as velocity approaches c, as gravitational field approaches zero."

5. **Self-as-observer embedding** — SOURCE: "Einstein placed himself inside the thought experiment ('I am riding the light wave,' 'I am in the falling elevator'). First-person embodiment of the scenario was a consistent feature."

6. **Theory collision scanning** — INFERENCE: The four-step structure (construct extreme scenario → apply existing laws → identify contradiction → infer resolution principle) represents a systematic methodology extractable from Einstein's documented thought experiments.

**Anti-patterns Avoided:**

- SOURCE: Einstein did not begin from mathematical formalism and derive toward physical intuition — he inverted this sequence (insight → formalism, not formalism → insight).
- SOURCE: Einstein did not accept domain boundaries as explanatory barriers (terrestrial vs. celestial mechanics were unified through his thought experiments).

**Tier Assignment Summary:** All traits Tier 1 (directly implementable as concrete LLM behavioral instructions with documented evidence from source material).

### 1.2 Feynman: First-Principles Decomposition

**Documented Cognitive Traits:**

1. **Name/concept separation** — SOURCE: "I learned very early the difference between knowing the name of something and knowing something." SOURCE: Bird metaphor demonstrating that names are social facts while understanding is structural facts.

2. **Jargon removal as diagnostic** — SOURCE: "If you have to test your knowledge about the concept of something, say energy, then without using the word 'energy', try to rephrase what you know about it in your own language." SOURCE: "We often talk in fact-deficient, obfuscating generalities to cover up our lack of understanding."

3. **Gap-identification via teaching attempt** — SOURCE: The four-step technique (write for sixth-grader → find breakdown points → return to source material for gaps → rebuild until flow without jargon) documented from fs.blog.

4. **Derivation vs. axiom classification** — SOURCE: From Feynman Lectures preface: "When new ideas came in, I would try either to deduce them if they were deducible or to explain that it was a new idea... and which was not supposed to be provable."

5. **First-principles orientation from childhood** — SOURCE: Wikipedia documents that when repairing radios as a child, Feynman "would analyze the issues theoretically and arrive at the solutions" — reasoning from physics principles rather than applying memorized patterns.

**Anti-patterns Avoided:**

- SOURCE: Feynman actively rejected fluency in jargon as evidence of understanding.
- SOURCE: Feynman distinguished derivable conclusions from axiomatic starting points explicitly in teaching.

**Tier Assignment Summary:** All traits Tier 1 (concrete behavioral instructions documented with direct quotes and examples).

### 1.3 Feynman: Error-Seeking and Calibration

**Documented Cognitive Traits:**

1. **Self-deception as primary threat** — SOURCE: "The first principle is that you must not fool yourself — and you are the easiest person to fool." SOURCE: Cargo Cult Science speech (Caltech 1974).

2. **Differential effort detection** — SOURCE: Millikan oil drop example: "When they got a number that was too high above Millikan's, they thought something must be wrong — and they would look for and find a reason why something might be wrong. When they got a number closer to Millikan's value they didn't look so hard."

3. **Active disconfirmation reporting** — SOURCE: "You must report everything that you think might make it invalid — not only what you think is right about it."

4. **Symmetric scrutiny enforcement** — INFERENCE: Derived from Millikan example — calibration requires detecting and correcting asymmetric scrutiny applied to confirming vs. disconfirming results.

5. **Cargo cult detection** — SOURCE: "Follow all the apparent precepts and forms of scientific investigation, but they're missing something essential" — surface structure without epistemic discipline.

**Anti-patterns Avoided:**

- SOURCE: Differential scrutiny of results based on expectation alignment (Millikan example).
- SOURCE: Reporting confirming evidence while omitting disconfirming evidence.

**Tier Assignment Summary:** All traits Tier 1 (directly implementable behavioral instructions with documented evidence).

### 1.4 Newton: Analogical Transfer Across Domains

**Documented Cognitive Traits:**

1. **Structural similarity identification** — SOURCE: Wikipedia confirmed Newton as a "Janusian thinker, someone who could mix and combine seemingly disparate fields." SOURCE: Terrestrial→celestial transfer documented in Principia.

2. **Formal rule as permission structure** — SOURCE: Rule 2 (Causal Uniformity): "If one cause is assigned to a natural effect, then the same cause so far as possible must be assigned to natural effects of the same kind." SOURCE: Rule 3 (Universal Induction) from Principia.

3. **Predictive test as validation** — SOURCE: Newton derived Kepler's laws, tidal mechanics, comet trajectories from single gravitational law transferred from terrestrial observation — predictive success validated analogy.

4. **Domain boundary refusal** — INFERENCE: Newton refused to treat domain boundaries as explanatory barriers. When phenomena in domain B resembled phenomena in domain A in formal structure, he treated the causal explanation of A as a hypothesis for B — then tested it.

5. **Deep structural comparison** — INFERENCE: For any phenomenon, explicitly extract its formal structure (input/output relationships, feedback loops, rate laws, conservation principles) independently of domain labels.

**Anti-patterns Avoided:**

- SOURCE: Newton abolished Aristotle's division between sublunar (imperfect, changeable) and celestial (perfect, eternal) realms — rejected domain boundaries as explanatory barriers.

**Tier Assignment Summary:** Traits 1-3 are Tier 1; Traits 4-5 are Tier 2 (inferred methodologies without explicit documentation of Newton's internal cognitive process).

### 1.5 Turing: Formalization of Intuition

**Documented Cognitive Traits:**

1. **Primitive decomposition** — SOURCE: Turing modeled the formal machine directly on human computational processes: read one symbol, finite mental states, write/erase symbols, move attention. SOURCE: Wikipedia Turing machine construction method.

2. **Constructive specification** — SOURCE: "Build 'a thicket of abbreviations' and standardized descriptions, requiring every informal operation to be expressed as a deterministic rule."

3. **Vagueness extraction** — SOURCE: Turing's 1936 paper replaced the informal concept "effective computability" with a precise formal model. SOURCE: Turing test operationalized "machine thinking" as behavioral indistinguishability.

4. **Self-reference test** — SOURCE: The diagonal argument applied the formal model to itself — halting problem revealed structural boundary that informal concept concealed.

5. **Minimal model construction** — INFERENCE: Build smallest formal system that reproduces essential behavior, test on edge cases, identify where model produces contradictions.

**Anti-patterns Avoided:**

- SOURCE: Turing did not argue about informal concepts — he replaced them with precise formal models.
- SOURCE: Tolerance for vagueness in informal concepts — Turing forced every step into symbolic form.

**Tier Assignment Summary:** Traits 1-4 are Tier 1; Trait 5 is Tier 2 (inferred from documented methodology but not explicitly stated as a cognitive operation by Turing).

3. **"Ten steps ahead" derivation** — SOURCE: Los Alamos colleagues observed von Neumann could "jump ten steps ahead" in calculation while others worked through first step.

## Section 3: Unique Trait AI-Amplification Analyses

### 3.1 Golden Rule for Disconfirming Evidence (Darwin)

**Cognitive Distinction:** Most evidence-gathering is confirmation-seeking. Darwin's golden rule is an *asymmetric* memory intervention — disconfirming observations are captured immediately because they are systematically forgotten. The asymmetry is the key: confirming evidence is remembered; disconfirming evidence fades.

**Computational Equivalent:** An LLM does not experience motivated forgetting, but it experiences *anchoring on recent framing*. The analog: maintain an explicit running list of disconfirming observations rather than implicitly down-weighting them when they become inconvenient.

**Maximum Theoretical Amplification:** 
- Human: Can hold ~7 disconfirming observations in working memory; biological memory decays
- AI: Can hold unlimited disconfirming observations; explicit persistence prevents decay
- Amplification factor: 10x+ in evidence density

---

### 3.2 Differential Effort Detection (Feynman)

**Cognitive Distinction:** The Millikan example reveals that bias is not just in *what* evidence is collected, but in *how hard* you look at it. Confirming results get cursory examination; disconfirming results get scrutinized for errors.

**Computational Equivalent:** Run a symmetric scrutiny pass: generate the three strongest arguments against the conclusion, apply at least as much effort to each as was applied to confirming arguments.

**Maximum Theoretical Amplification:**
- Human: Effort is finite and motivated; scrutiny requires will
- AI: Can apply uniform effort mechanically without motivated suppression
- Amplification factor: Infinite (eliminates the asymmetry entirely)

---

### 3.3 Visual Simulation with Failure Detection (Tesla)

**Cognitive Distinction:** Tesla's mental model was *dynamic* — he ran the device in his mind and noted imbalances. This is simulation, not imagery. The distinction is crucial: imagery represents; simulation predicts.

**Computational Equivalent:** Given a system design, run it step-by-step in language: "At time T=0, component A is in state S. What is the state at T=1?" Continue until failure mode, steady state, or output condition. Load testing via scenario generation.

**Maximum Theoretical Amplification:**
- Human: Limited by domain knowledge completeness; fatigue in simulation runs
- AI: Can simulate systematically across all parameter spaces; no fatigue
- Amplification factor: Can run 1000x more simulation variants

---

### 3.4 Four-Stage Incubation Model (Poincaré)

**Cognitive Distinction:** Poincaré documented that illumination follows "voluntary effort which has appeared absolutely fruitless." The impasse is required — continued effort on exhausted paths prevents incubation.

**Computational Equivalent:** True incubation (background processing) cannot be directly implemented in LLMs. But the functional equivalent can: directed peripheral activation. Explicitly switch to least-examined aspects, follow association chains without filtering for relevance.

**Maximum Theoretical Amplification:**
- Human: Incubation is unreliable; depends on sleep/waking states
- AI: Can implement directed diversification mechanically and reliably
- Amplification factor: Can trigger "incubation-equivalent" on demand

---

### 3.5 Constraint-Naming + Domain-Switch (Archimedes)

**Cognitive Distinction:** Archimedes didn't just solve problems in other domains — he *explicitly identified* the constraint that made the problem hard, then found a domain where that constraint didn't exist.

**Computational Equivalent:** For any problem, name the blocking constraint. Survey multiple domains for where that constraint doesn't apply. Translate problem, solve in new domain, translate back.

**Maximum Theoretical Amplification:**
- Human: Limited by domain visibility — can only translate to domains you know
- AI: Has knowledge of multiple formal domains simultaneously; can survey in parallel
- Amplification factor: 20x+ in domain inventory

---

### 3.6 Self-as-Observer Embedding (Einstein)

**Cognitive Distinction:** Einstein placed himself *inside* thought experiments ("I am riding the light wave"). This is first-person embodiment, not third-person analysis. The perspective shift is structural.

**Computational Equivalent:** Construct scenarios with "I am the observer who..." framing. Run from multiple observer positions. The frame where contradiction vanishes reveals invariant structure.

**Maximum Theoretical Amplification:**
- Human: Limited imagination bandwidth; scenarios stay near familiar experience
- AI: Can generate systematically across all observer frames; not limited by experience
- Amplification factor: Infinite (all frames possible)

---

### 3.7 Jargon-Removal as Diagnostic (Feynman)

**Cognitive Distinction:** Feynman identified that technical vocabulary masks the absence of understanding. The test: explain to a sixth-grader without technical vocabulary. If you cannot, you do not understand.

**Computational Equivalent:** Explicitly ban domain vocabulary in explanation-generation. Force reconstruction from structure. At which step does explanation break down? Those are understanding gaps.

**Maximum Theoretical Amplification:**
- Human: Fluency illusion makes jargon feel like understanding
- AI: Can mechanically enforce vocabulary constraints; identify gaps precisely
- Amplification factor: Can generate at 5 vocabulary levels and identify exactly where understanding dissolves

---

### 3.8 Drawing as Disambiguation (Da Vinci)

**Cognitive Distinction:** Drawing forces precise specification. You cannot draw "approximately" a branch point — you must commit to the exact topology. Visual rendering resolves ambiguities that verbal description allows to remain vague.

**Computational Equivalent:** For any qualitatively described structure, demand precise specification: exact topology, exact relationship types, exact boundary conditions. The disambiguation step is the cognitive equivalent of drawing.

**Maximum Theoretical Amplification:**
- Human: Visual system limitations; drawing skill required
- AI: Can demand precise specification for all concepts; no visual limitation
- Amplification factor: All concepts can be "drawn" (precisely specified), not just visualizable ones
