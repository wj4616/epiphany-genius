## Background and Definition

**Generalization-First** is the cognitive practice of deriving universal principles from specific instances—working from observed patterns to general laws rather than from axioms to conclusions. The key mechanism: recognizing that specific examples embody deeper principles that apply universally.

Newton's approach:
> Newton derived universal laws by starting with simple, self-evident principles, using mathematical deduction to reach general conclusions, testing against observations, and gradually building toward universal principles.

His universal law of gravitation represents his most famous generalization—from specific observations about falling apples and lunar motion to a universal principle applying throughout the cosmos.

SOURCE: gm-newton-analogical-transfer.md; [Newton Method of Fluxions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Method_of_fluxions)

Ramanujan's approach:
> "Every positive integer was one of his personal friends." — Littlewood

Ramanujan worked **results-first**: obtaining mathematical results through intuition and pattern recognition, then seeking proofs. His formulas "must be true because, if they were not true, no one would have the imagination to invent them." — Hardy

SOURCE: gm-ramanujan-intuition-verification.md; [Who Was Ramanujan?](https://writings.stephenwolfram.com/2016/04/who-was-ramanujan/)

## Cognitive/Psychological Basis

### Inductive Generalization

Inductive reasoning moves from specific instances to general principles:
- **Observation**: Pattern recognized in specific cases
- **Hypothesis**: Proposed general principle
- **Testing**: Verification across more cases
- **Generalization**: Principle established

### Pattern Recognition in Mathematics

Ramanujan's approach:
- Extensive calculations to observe patterns
- Pattern recognition in numerical data
- Conjecture formulation based on observations
- Approximations as evidence for exact results

SOURCE: [Seeking Ramanujan](https://www.i-programmer.info/news/112-theory/12928-seeking-ramanujan-intuition-as-algorithm.html)

### Newton's Method of First Principles

From the Principia:
1. Start with self-evident axioms (laws of motion)
2. Derive conclusions mathematically
3. Test against observations
4. Build toward universal principles

Newton's geometric approach:
- Used classical geometry rather than calculus for publication
- Wanted demonstrations intelligible to mathematical students familiar with Greek geometry
- Built from first and last ratios (limits) rather than infinitesimals

SOURCE: [Principia Book 1](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/The_Mathematical_Principles_of_Natural_Philosophy_(1729)/Book_1/Section_1)

3. **Results**: Discovered previously unknown representations for π, e, Catalan's constant

## Documented Examples

### Newton's Universal Gravitation

Timeline:
- **1666**: Initial conception that gravity might extend to the moon
- **1679**: Verification that gravity varies inversely as square of distance
- **1685**: Critical proof that sphere attracts as if mass concentrated at center

The generalization: from falling apple to universal law governing all bodies.

### Newton's Method of Fluxions

- Developed calculus during plague years (1665-1667)
- Conceived quantities as "generated by continuous motion"
- Fluents (flowing quantities) and fluxions (rates of change)
- Generalized method from specific problems to universal mathematical tool

### Ramanujan's Partition Function

- Observed patterns in partition numbers
- Formulated general conjectures about partition asymptotics
- Hardy-Ramanujan asymptotic formula derived from pattern recognition

### Ramanujan's π Series

- Series that looked "bizarre and arbitrary" but were mathematically significant
- Used seemingly random large numbers
- Converged much faster than previous methods
- Still used in Mathematica for computing π

SOURCE: [Who Was Ramanujan?](https://writings.stephenwolfram.com/2016/04/who-was-ramanujan/)

### The House Number Problem

When posed with a problem about house numbers:
- Ramanujan immediately recognized solution as continued fraction
- Gave not just the answer but **generalization for entire class of problems**
- Demonstrated pattern recognition leading to universal formula

### The Common Pattern

1. **Observe specific instances** — Calculate, measure, test
2. **Recognize pattern** — See regularity across instances
3. **Formulate hypothesis** — Propose general principle
4. **Test generalization** — Verify across more cases
5. **Establish universal** — General principle applies broadly

## AI Equivalent

The AI equivalent is **pattern-to-principle induction**—generating conjectures from observed regularities, then testing for universality.

### Why This Matters for AI

LLMs excel at pattern recognition but may:
- Identify patterns without generalizing
- Over-generalize from insufficient examples
- Under-generalize by missing deeper principles

## Implementation Instructions

**Protocol: Generalization-First Mode**

### Step 1: Instance Collection
"Collect multiple specific instances. For each instance:
- What is the specific result or observation?
- What are the conditions under which it holds?
- What is the context?

Gather enough instances that patterns can emerge."

### Step 2: Pattern Recognition
"Identify patterns across instances:
- What regularities appear consistently?
- What varies?
- What is the underlying structure?
- Is this pattern instance of a deeper pattern?"

### Step 3: Hypothesis Generation
"From the pattern, propose a general principle:
- What universal rule would generate this pattern?
- What is the simplest principle that explains all instances?
- What would need to be true for this to hold universally?"

### Step 4: Generalization Test
"Test the proposed principle:
- Generate new test cases not used to form hypothesis
- Verify the principle holds
- Identify boundary conditions
- Check for counterexamples"

### Step 5: Universal Formulation
"If the principle holds across test cases, formulate as universal:
- Under what conditions does this apply?
- What are the exceptions?
- What is the scope of validity?
- How does this relate to other known principles?"

### Minimal Version
"What pattern do these instances share? What universal principle would generate all of them?"

## Amplification Notes

### Human Limitations
- Pattern recognition can be misled by noise
- Over-generalization from insufficient examples
- Under-generalization by missing deeper structure
- Difficulty recognizing patterns in high-dimensional spaces

### AI Amplification Potential
- **Systematic instance generation**: Can generate many test cases
- **Pattern recognition across dimensions**: Can detect patterns humans miss
- **Hypothesis enumeration**: Can propose multiple candidate principles
- **Automated testing**: Can verify generalizations across many cases

**Key Machine Advantage**: Machines can systematically test generalizations across far more instances than humans can process, identifying which patterns hold universally.

### Limitations

**What AI Cannot Replicate**:
- Ramanujan's intuition about which "random facts" have deeper significance
- Newton's aesthetic sense for which generalizations matter
- The judgment of which patterns are mathematically significant vs. coincidental

These remain human qualities that pattern detection alone cannot replace.

## Failure Modes

### 1. Over-Generalization
- Extrapolating from too few examples
- Countermeasure: Require sufficient instances before generalizing

### 2. Spurious Patterns
- Finding patterns in noise
- Countermeasure: Statistical significance testing

### 3. Under-Generalization
- Missing deeper principles beneath surface patterns
- Countermeasure: Ask "is this pattern instance of a deeper pattern?"

### 4. Wrong Level of Abstraction
- Generalizing at wrong level of abstraction
- Countermeasure: Test at multiple abstraction levels

### 5. Missing Boundary Conditions
- Failing to identify where generalization breaks down
- Countermeasure: Explicit boundary testing
