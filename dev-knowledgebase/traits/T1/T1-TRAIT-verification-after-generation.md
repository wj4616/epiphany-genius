## Background and Definition

**Verification After Generation** is the cognitive practice of systematically separating the generation of candidate solutions from their verification—a two-phase structure where hypothesis generation occurs first, followed by independent verification. This universal pattern appears across 6 of 12 documented geniuses, making it one of the most frequent cognitive traits.

The practice involves:
- **Generation phase**: Producing candidate solutions, hypotheses, or outputs without immediate filtering
- **Separation**: A distinct phase boundary between generation and verification
- **Verification phase**: Testing, checking, or proving the generated candidates
- **Independent verification**: Verification conducted without contamination from generation assumptions

SOURCE: Across genius-minds files, the generation-verification separation is documented explicitly in Ramanujan-Hardy collaboration, Archimedes' mechanical method, Poincaré's four-stage model, Feynman's calibration, Darwin's pre-publication objection exhaustion, and Turing's formalization.

## Cognitive/Psychological Basis

### The Monitor-Generate-Verify Framework

Recent research formalizing metacognitive theory identifies a three-phase structure:
1. **Monitor** — Capture metacognitive experiences before reasoning begins
2. **Generate** — Produce candidate solutions
3. **Verify** — Evaluate validity independently

This addresses the **"prefix dominance trap"** where models commit early to suboptimal reasoning paths, causing ~20% accuracy loss.

SOURCE: [Monitor-Generate-Verify (MGV): Formalising Metacognitive Theory](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.04341v1)

### Neuroscience of Error Monitoring

The brain's error monitoring system involves:

**Key Brain Regions:**
- **Medial frontal cortex (MFC)** — including pre-SMA and ACC
- **Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)** — conflict monitoring
- **Ventral striatum** — signed prediction errors
- **Insula** — unsigned (absolute) prediction errors

**Error-Related Negativity (ERN):**
- EEG biomarker indexing error detection
- Generated by neurons in ACC and pre-SMA
- Occurs ~50-100ms after errors

**Core Mechanisms:**
- **Conflict monitoring** — detecting competition between response alternatives
- **Prediction error computation** — comparing expected vs. actual outcomes
- **Post-error slowing** — behavioral adjustment after errors

SOURCE: [Neurophysiological mechanisms of error monitoring](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41583-022-00670-w)

INFERENCE: The separation of generation and verification has neural correlates—the brain has dedicated error monitoring systems that operate independently of action generation.

### The Generate-Verify Asymmetry

A fundamental principle: **Verification is computationally easier than generation**

- Checking correctness requires less cognitive resources than producing correct output
- This asymmetry enables verification systems to work reliably even when generation systems fail
- DeepVerifier research demonstrates 12%-48% improvement in meta-evaluation F1 by leveraging this asymmetry

### Poincaré's Four-Stage Model

The model explicitly separates:
- **Preparation** — loading problem state
- **Incubation** — unconscious processing
- **Illumination** — unbidden insight (generation)
- **Verification** — conscious confirmation (verification)

Illumination is generation; verification is a distinct subsequent phase. Without verification, illuminations cannot be trusted.

SOURCE: gm-poincare-incubation.md

### Feynman's Error-Seeking

Feynman's reporting standard:
> "You must report everything that you think might make it invalid — not only what you think is right about it."

The calibration process: generate results, then seek errors with equal effort as was spent confirming.

## AI Equivalent

The AI equivalent is the **Chain-of-Verification (CoVe)** and related generator-verifier architectures.

### Chain-of-Verification (CoVe)

A four-stage pipeline for reducing hallucinations:
1. **Baseline Response Generation** — Initial draft answer
2. **Verification Planning** — Generate targeted verification questions
3. **Independent Verification Execution** — Answer each question independently (critical for avoiding confirmation bias)
4. **Final Verified Response** — Synthesize and correct factual errors

Key finding: **Factored CoVe** (most effective variant) fully isolates verification from baseline response.

SOURCE: [Chain-of-Verification (CoVe) - AI Agent Knowledge Base](https://agentwiki.org/chain_of_verification)

### Generator-Verifier Systems (EVE)

The EVE framework:
- **Generator** produces candidate actions
- **Verifier** proposes action refinements
- **Action Incorporator** fuses aggregated verifier output into base policy
- Consistently improves task success rates

SOURCE: [EVE: A Generator-Verifier System for Generative Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.21430)

### Self-Grounded Verification (SGV)

Addresses **"agreement bias"** — MLLMs' tendency to over-validate their own outputs:
- First elicit broad priors about desired behavior (unconditional generation)
- Then evaluate candidate trajectory conditioned on self-generated priors
- **25 percentage point improvement** in failure detection
- **14pp accuracy gain**

SOURCE: [Self-Grounded Verification](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.11662)

## Implementation Instructions

**Protocol: Generate-Then-Verify Mode**

### Step 1: Generation Phase
"Generate candidate solutions without filtering. Do not verify during generation. List all plausible candidates, including those you are uncertain about."

Prompt template:
```
## Generation Phase
Task: [problem description]

Instructions:
- Generate ALL plausible solutions
- Do NOT filter or verify during generation
- Include candidates you are uncertain about
- List each candidate separately

Candidates:
1. [candidate 1]
2. [candidate 2]
...
```

### Step 2: Phase Separation
"Generation phase complete. Now entering verification phase. Do not return to generation."

Explicit phase boundary is critical—prevents verification from contaminating generation.

### Step 3: Independent Verification
"For each candidate generated, verify independently. Do not use assumptions from generation."

Prompt template:
```
## Verification Phase
For each candidate:

Candidate: [X]
Verification questions:
1. Does this satisfy all constraints?
2. Are there any contradictions?
3. What would disconfirm this?
4. What are the failure modes?

Verification result: [PASS / FAIL / NEEDS MODIFICATION]
```

### Step 4: Final Synthesis
"Synthesize verification results into final answer. Include confidence levels and remaining uncertainties."

### Minimal Version
"First generate all candidates. Then, separately, verify each. Do not mix generation and verification."

## Amplification Notes

### Human Limitations
- Confirmation bias operates through differential memory decay
- Generated solutions feel familiar, biasing verification
- Expertise can reduce skepticism toward self-generated outputs
- Verification effort is asymmetrically distributed (more effort on confirming than disconfirming)

### AI Amplification Potential
- **Independent verification guaranteed**: Systems can be designed to verify without access to generation assumptions
- **Uniform verification effort**: No motivated reasoning bias
- **Multiple verification passes**: Can run verification many times without fatigue
- **Steel-manning verification**: Can actively generate strongest counterarguments

SOURCE: Research on self-verification dilemma shows AI models engage in recheck behaviors, but 85-95% are confirmatory. Proper separation protocols reduce this bias.

## Failure Modes

### 1. Verification Contamination
When verification phase has access to generation assumptions:
- Confirmation bias seeps into verification
- Critical flaws are missed
- Verification becomes rubber-stamping

Countermeasure: Fully factored verification (no access to generation context)

### 2. Insufficient Verification Effort
- Generated solutions accepted too quickly
- "Agreement bias" in self-verification
- Over-verification of confirming evidence

SOURCE: Self-Grounded Verification research identifies this as a key failure mode

### 3. Premature Verification
- Verification begins before generation is complete
- Stops exploration of alternative candidates
- Locks in on first plausible solution

Countermeasure: Explicit phase separation with generation-complete signal

### 4. Verification Without Independence
When verifiers share assumptions with generators:
- Blind spots transfer from generation to verification
- Errors that occurred in generation are not caught

Countermeasure: Independent verification systems or separate verification prompts
